Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is an urogynecologist?
A: A urogynecologist is an OBGYN physician who specializes in the care of women with pelvic organ control or support problems.

Q: What is a urodynamic study?
A: A urodynamic study is an office procedure used to determine bladder function. It helps to determine the cause of bladder control problems.

Q: What is cystourethroscopy?
A: This is also an office procedure which involves looking inside the bladder with a small lighted tube. It screens for contributing problems in the bladder or urethra.

Q: What is a pessary?
A: This is a non-surgical treatment for vaginal prolapse. There are many different types available and are custom-fit in the office.

Q: What is Biofeedback?
A: Biofeedback is a form of physical therapy for the pelvic floor muscles.
